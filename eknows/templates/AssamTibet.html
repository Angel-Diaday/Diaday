Ten Most Powerful Earthquake in Earth History

• Assam-Tibet Earthquake (1950) - 8.6 Magnitude

Date: August 15, 1950
Time: 14:09 UTC (19:39 in local time)
Epicenter: 20 miles beneath the town of Rami in Tibet, India
Summary of Damages:1,500 to 3,000 people died as a result of the quake. After the earthquake, a large landslide blocked the Subansiri River. The landslide broke several villages were flooded, and 536 people were killed. The earthquake also created about five million homeless people.
