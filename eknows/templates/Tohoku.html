Ten Most Powerful Earthquake in Earth History

• Tohoku Earthquake (2011) - 9.1 Magnitude

Date: March 11, 2011
Time: 05:46 UTC (14:46 in local time)
Epicenter: Coast of Fukushima, about 200 kilometers north of Tokyo.
Summary of Damages: The earthquake triggered a massive tsunami with waves up to 40.5 meters high. Airports, roads, and railroads were destroyed. 127,290 buildings are totally collapsed, 272,788 buildings are half collapsed, and 747,989 buildings were partially damaged and a dam collapsed.
