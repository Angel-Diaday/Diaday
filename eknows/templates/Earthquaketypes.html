Types of Earthquake

Tectonic Earthquake

It occurs when the earth's crust breaks due to geological forces on rocks and adjoining plates that cause physical and chemical changes.

Volcanic Earthquake

An earthquake results from tectonic forces which occur in conjunction with volcanic activity.

Explosive Earthquake

Small earthquakes in underground caverns and mines are caused by seismic waves produced from the explosion of rock on the surface.

Explosion Earthquake

An earthquake is the result of the detonation of a nuclear and/or chemical device.
