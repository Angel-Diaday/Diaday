Types of Plate Boundaries

Convergent Boundary

When two plates come together, this is called a convergent boundary. The impact of a colliding plate can cause edges of the plate to bulge into the mountains. The Pacific Ring of Fire is an example of a convergent plate boundary.

Divergent Boundary

When the plates seperate, it referred as the divergent plate edge occurs. An example of a structural plate boundary is the Mid-Atlantic Ridge.

Transform Boundary

A transform boundary where the two plates slides against each other in the same plane. Strike slip failures occur at transformation boundaries. For example, a system of strike-slip failures forms a transformation boundary for San Andres fault.
