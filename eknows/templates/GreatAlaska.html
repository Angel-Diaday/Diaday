Ten Most Powerful Earthquake in Earth History

• Great Alaska Earthquake (1964) - 9.2 Magnitude

Date: March 27, 1964
Time: 03:36 UTC (17:36 in local time)
Epicenter: 10 km east of College Fiord, approximately 90 km west of Valdez, 120 km east of Anchorage.
Summary of Damages: The earthquake caused a 8.2-meter-high tsunami and killed 23 of the 68 people who lived there. The port and wharf of Port Valdez collapsed, killing 30 people. A total of 139 people are said to have died. The quake itself killed 15 people and the tsunami in Alaska is 106 people.
