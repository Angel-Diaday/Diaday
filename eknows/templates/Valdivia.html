Ten Most Powerful Earthquake in Earth History

• Valdivia Earthquake (1960) - 9.0 Magnitude

Date: May 22, 1960
Time: 19:11 GMT (15:11 in local time)
Epicenter: 100 miles off the coast of Chile, parallel to the city of Valdivia
Summary of Damages: Main tsunami devastated the Chilean coast and crossed the Pacific Ocean. Total death toll is estimated to be 1,000 to 6,000. About 3,000 people were injured. It lost two million homes and killed about 1,655. The economic damage amounted to US 550 million dollars.
