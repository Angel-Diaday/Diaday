Ten Most Powerful Earthquake in Earth History

• Sumatra Earthquake (2012) - 8.6 Magnitude

Date: April 11, 2012
Time: 08:38 UTC (15:38 in local time)
Epicenter: 215 kilometers, southwest of Padang, West Sumatra
Summary of Damages: Unusually strong earthquakes on the plate, a largest strike-slip earthquakes ever recorded. It occurred very far from the place of residence and did not have a destructive tsunami. Ten deaths and 12 injuries were reported. Most of them were caused by panic and or heart attack.
