Top Ten Strongest Earthquake to hit the Philippines

• 1645: Magnitude 7.5 in Luzon

Date and Time: November 30, 1645, 8:00 PM PST
Epicenter: Produced by the San Manuel and Gabaldon Faults in Nueva Ecija
Description: It is one of the most destructive earthquake since the Spanish regime. Manila was in totally wrecked were the bodies were buried under the ruins.
Damages: 600 Spanish people were killed, and 3,000 Spanish people were injured. Manila was entirely damaged especially the newly constructed cathedrals, and residential villas are being crumpled into pieces.
